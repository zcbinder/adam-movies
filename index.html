The Case Study
Adam gave Ocean's Eleven zero stars. He gave Ocean's Twelve five stars and Ocean's Thirteen five stars. He watched all three. He is not confused. He simply has standards, and those standards involve sequels being objectively better than their originals regardless of critical consensus, box office performance, or basic narrative coherence. He once threw pool cleaning chemicals over a fence at his own bachelor party. He placed a glass beer bottle gently into a pool to watch it sink. His decision-making framework is internally consistent. It just doesn't map to anything external.

He also gave Dante's Peak (1997) five stars. Dante's Peak has a 33% on Rotten Tomatoes. It is a movie about a volcano. Adam has Premier Platinum status with United Airlines. He has seen the world. He has processed this and concluded that Dante's Peak is excellent cinema.
About Adam
The man behind the ratings. Turning 40. Being roasted accordingly.

Origin

Adam is from Racine, WI. He will say Burlington, WI...but those are the same.

Academic Career

Dropped out of UW–Stout. Then attended Marquette University for years — not as a student, but as a party host, charging enrolled students for cups. Attended more Marquette basketball games than most actual students. Left without a degree. Left with connections.

Professional Life

Works in the spring manufacturing industry. Featured writer in SPRINGS Magazine. Maintains an active LinkedIn presence — whether sincere or performance art remains unclear. Holds United MileagePlus Premier Platinum status. Loves a United lounge.

Section I — Background and Evidence Base

The subject came to the attention of this department following a referral from the Forensic Media Analysis Unit. The sole evidence available for this evaluation consists of the subject's Netflix DVD rental history spanning 2009 through December 2023: 1,566 discrete rentals, 3,440 individual film ratings, and zero (0) written reviews. This is noted.

Dr. M. Freeman was assigned to this case. He has agreed to evaluate the subject despite acknowledging, for the record, that he appears in several films the subject rented at elevated frequency. Dr. Freeman has filed a partial conflict-of-interest disclosure. It was filed silently, without elaboration, consistent with the subject's own communication style.

The subject's first Netflix disc was I Love You, Man, rented September 25, 2009. His 50th rental was Dante's Peak — which he awarded 5 stars. This tells you nearly everything you need to know. His 500th rental was The Kings of Summer (2013). He did not slow down.

Section II — Behavioral Indicators
Finding 1: Oppositional Rating Disorder

Subject has systematically assigned 0 stars — the minimum possible rating — to the following widely acclaimed films: The Lord of the Rings trilogy, Star Wars (all episodes), True Grit, Ocean's Eleven, Avatar, A Hijacking, and Pumping Iron. Simultaneously, subject assigned the maximum rating of 5 stars to: Geostorm (2017, Rotten Tomatoes: 13%), Crank, Dante's Peak, The Expendables, John Wick, Taken, and Everybody Loves Raymond (all nine seasons, all disc variants, without exception). This evaluative framework operates entirely independent of critical or cultural consensus. The subject is not confused. He has simply decided.

Finding 2: Genre-Based Threat Fixation

Of 1,566 rentals, approximately 483 (31%) involve Action Thrillers or Crime Thrillers. An additional 153 rentals are Period Pieces. This incongruent pairing — catastrophic present-day violence alongside costumed historical drama — suggests a subject who maintains a dual aesthetic life. He wants things to blow up, but he also has a soft spot for the 18th century. Both impulses have been acted upon repeatedly and without apology.

Finding 3: Extreme Postal Content Acquisition

The subject rented Cannibal Holocaust, The Human Centipede 2: Full Sequence, and Nymphomaniac (Volumes I and II) through the United States Postal Service. These are, to be clear, legal rentals. They are also extraordinary choices. The subject gave no written explanation for any of these decisions. See also: Finding 6 (Silence Under Pressure).

Finding 4: Raymond Syndrome (Proposed Diagnosis)

At time of report, the subject's active Netflix queue contained eight (8) consecutive entries of Everybody Loves Raymond, Seasons 2 through 8, spanning multiple discs per season. The subject has previously rented and rated the entirety of Season 1. He is re-engaging. Queued between Season 3, Disc 4 and Season 4, Disc 1 with no explanation: The Man Who Killed Hitler and Then the Bigfoot. No clarifying note was left. None is expected.

Finding 5: The September 2014 Incident

Subject rented seventeen (17) DVDs in September 2014 — more than double his average monthly volume and a statistical outlier across his entire 14-year rental history. Forensic records do not indicate what precipitated this event. The subject did not write a review about it. Investigative status: open.

Finding 6: Silence Under Pressure

Subject generated 3,440 ratings across 14 years of active membership, placing him in the top 10% of Netflix raters nationally. He has written zero (0) reviews. This is a statistically anomalous silence for someone operating at this volume of expressed opinion. The subject knows what he thinks. He has chosen not to explain it. This department considers that decision a form of statement.

Finding 7: Morgan Freeman Dependency

The most-rented actor in the subject's history is Morgan Freeman. The most-rented actress is Cate Blanchett. Dr. Freeman notes this finding, declines to analyze it further, and has requested that this section be reviewed by an independent evaluator. That request has been denied. The finding stands.
