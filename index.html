The Case Study
Adam gave Ocean's Eleven zero stars. He gave Ocean's Twelve five stars and Ocean's Thirteen five stars. He watched all three. He is not confused. He simply has standards, and those standards involve sequels being objectively better than their originals regardless of critical consensus, box office performance, or basic narrative coherence. He once threw pool cleaning chemicals over a fence at his own bachelor party. He placed a glass beer bottle gently into a pool to watch it sink. His decision-making framework is internally consistent. It just doesn't map to anything external.

He also gave Dante's Peak (1997) five stars. Dante's Peak has a 33% on Rotten Tomatoes. It is a movie about a volcano. Adam has Premier Platinum status with United Airlines. He has seen the world. He has processed this and concluded that Dante's Peak is excellent cinema.
About Adam
The man behind the ratings. Turning 40. Being roasted accordingly.

Origin

Adam is from Racine, WI. He will say Burlington, WI...but those are the same.

Academic Career

Dropped out of UW–Stout. Then attended Marquette University for years — not as a student, but as a party host, charging enrolled students for cups. Attended more Marquette basketball games than most actual students. Left without a degree. Left with connections.

Works in the spring manufacturing industry. Featured writer in SPRINGS Magazine. Maintains an active LinkedIn presence — whether sincere or performance art remains unclear. Holds United MileagePlus Premier Platinum status. Loves a United lounge.

Section I — Background and Evidence Base

The subject came to the attention of this department following a referral from the Forensic Media Analysis Unit, which flagged his rental profile as "statistically unprecedented and emotionally concerning." Over fourteen years, the subject rented 1,566 DVDs and generated 3,440 individual ratings. He watched more films than most professional critics. He arrived at worse conclusions than all of them.

Dr. M. Freeman was assigned to this case under protest. He has acknowledged appearing in several films the subject rented at elevated frequency, and has filed a conflict-of-interest disclosure. He filed it, as one might expect of a man who has read this file, in complete silence.

The subject's first Netflix disc was I Love You, Man, rented September 25, 2009. His 50th rental was Dante's Peak — a 1997 volcano film with a 33% on Rotten Tomatoes — which he awarded 5 stars. He had rented 49 other films before this and absorbed nothing. His 500th rental was The Kings of Summer (2013). He did not slow down. He did not improve.

Section II — Behavioral Indicators
Finding 1: Oppositional Rating Disorder

Subject zero-starred the following: The Lord of the Rings: The Fellowship of the Ring, all six Star Wars films, Ocean's Eleven, Avatar, True Grit, A Hijacking, Milk, and Pumping Iron. He watched every one of them. He mailed them back. He ordered the next one. Meanwhile, he awarded 5 stars to Geostorm (Rotten Tomatoes: 13%), Dante's Peak (33%), Crank, The Expendables, and Taken — films in which the primary problem-solving methodology is violence and the secondary is more violence. The subject watched Ocean's Eleven, gave it the worst possible rating, and then immediately requested Ocean's Twelve, the inferior sequel, which he gave 5 stars. This is not a critical framework. This is a cry for help that has gone unanswered for fourteen years.

Finding 2: Genre-Based Threat Fixation

Of 1,566 rentals, approximately 483 (31%) are Action Thrillers or Crime Thrillers — films in which Gerard Butler, Liam Neeson, or Jason Statham is angry about something and intends to fix it. An additional 153 rentals are Period Pieces, which this department attributes to a second person in the household with different taste and the remote control occasionally. The subject rated his Action films significantly higher than his Period Pieces. He watched the Period Pieces anyway. This is called compromise, and the subject is apparently capable of it, though you would not know this from his star ratings.

Finding 3: Extreme Postal Content Acquisition

The subject rented Cannibal Holocaust, The Human Centipede 2: Full Sequence, and Nymphomaniac (Volumes I and II) through the United States Postal Service. A postal worker — a real person, with a family, trying to get through their day — handled these discs. They arrived in a cheerful red Netflix envelope. They were returned in the same envelope. The subject rated each of them and moved on without incident or apparent reflection. This department is not here to judge. We are, however, here to note it. It has been noted. It will continue to be noted.

Finding 4: Raymond Syndrome (Proposed Diagnosis)

At time of report, the subject's active queue contained eight consecutive entries of Everybody Loves Raymond, Seasons 2 through 8. He has previously completed Season 1. He is going back. He is going back to a show he has already finished, about a man whose family mildly irritates him, and he has queued all of it. Between Season 3, Disc 4 and Season 4, Disc 1, with no note and no apparent awareness of what he has done, the subject inserted The Man Who Killed Hitler and Then the Bigfoot. He will watch it, rate it, and return to Raymond. Season 4 is waiting. This department has filed this under mental health and moved on.

Finding 5: The September 2014 Incident

The subject rented seventeen (17) DVDs in September 2014 — more than double his average and a statistical outlier across his entire rental history. Something happened. The subject has not explained it. This department has theories, none of which reflect well on him. Investigative status: open, and likely to remain that way, because the subject will not be explaining anything.

Finding 6: The Five-Star Problem

The subject awarded five stars to 1,267 films — 37% of everything he watched. For context: Geostorm got five stars. Crank got five stars. 1 Cheerleader Camp got five stars. Aaah! Zombies!! got one star, which means even Adam has a floor, and it is apparently set just below zombie comedies. When 37% of all cinema is perfect, the word "perfect" has lost its meaning. The subject has devalued five stars to the point where receiving five stars from him is roughly equivalent to receiving a participation trophy from a man who hands them out to Gerard Butler films.

Finding 7: Morgan Freeman Dependency

The most-rented actor is Morgan Freeman. The most-rented actress is Scarlett Johansson. Dr. Freeman has reviewed this finding, declined to comment, and requested a transfer. The transfer was denied. He will finish the report.

Section VII — Occupational and Recreational Profile
Occupational Assessment (Inference-Based)

Two rentals per week for fourteen years implies a structured schedule and stable employment. The subject held a job. He came home. He watched films. The 153 Period Pieces in his history — genres he rated lower than his Action films — suggest he watched them in the presence of someone with different taste and the remote. He is employed, partnered, and apparently functional, which is more than this file would lead you to expect.

He gave five stars to 37% of everything he watched. This is the rating profile of a man who is easy to please at work, difficult to agree with about films, and probably described by colleagues as "a great guy" in a tone that suggests they have never discussed movies with him.

Recreational Profile

The subject watches films in which Liam Neeson has a particular set of skills, gives them five stars, and then watches The Notebook and also gives that five stars. He rented Cannibal Holocaust and The Human Centipede 2 through the mail with no apparent hesitation and no follow-up. When given unstructured time — see September 2014 — he fills it with cinema at twice his normal rate. His recreational life is his film life. This is fine. It is also all we have on him, and it is enough.
